Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the ThriveStack ↔ Google Ads integration.

What data does ThriveStack collect from my Google Ads campaigns?

There are two distinct sources — data pulled from Google Ads, and data ThriveStack collects about visitors who arrived via a Google ad click. These are different things.

Data pulled from Google Ads (synced daily via Airbyte)
click_view Your GCLID source — every ad click with its unique identifier, click timestamp, and campaign association. This is the join key that links a landing-page visit to a conversion event in Phase 2. Critical stream.
campaign_budget Budget allocated per campaign. Combined with conversion value, this is what makes ROAS (return on ad spend) calculable in ThriveStack reports.
customer Google Ads account metadata — account ID, name, currency, timezone. Needed to scope API calls correctly during OAuth setup and for multi-account support.
geographic_view
_with_metrics Cost, clicks, and impressions broken down by geography. Powers spend and performance reporting by region — useful for geo-targeting decisions.
Data ThriveStack collects when a visitor arrives from a Google ad
  • GCLID from the landing page URL — Google's auto-tagging appends ?gclid=… to your ad destination URL. ThriveStack's script reads and stores it in localStorage and a cookie on the visitor's first page load.
  • User identity at signup — when the anonymous visitor later signs up, ThriveStack links their email and account ID to the stored GCLID. This is your own data from your product's signup flow — not data from Google.
  • Lifecycle events — demo booked, trial started, first payment — captured from your product and linked to the GCLID. These are your product events, not Google Ads data.

ThriveStack does not collect browsing history, ad creative content, bid data, or any Google account information. The only data read from Google Ads itself is campaign metadata and click-level GCLID mappings from your own account.

What data does ThriveStack push back to Google Ads for feedback loops?

Two streams flow back to Google Ads — audience lists synced daily, and account-level conversion signals sent per event.

Customer Match — Audience Lists (daily sync)

Three lifecycle-based segments are pushed to Google Ads Audience Manager every day. Email addresses are SHA-256 hashed before sending — plain emails are never transmitted.

paying_customers All active paying accounts. Used to exclude from acquisition campaigns — stop spending on people already paying.
free_trial Accounts in trial, not yet paying. Used to target with conversion-focused ads to push them over the line.
churned Accounts that were paying and have since gone inactive. Used to target with win-back campaigns.
Offline Conversion Signals — per account event

When a tracked account reaches a lifecycle milestone, ThriveStack sends a conversion record to Google Ads. Each record contains:

GCLID The original click identifier captured when this account first landed from a Google ad. Google uses it to match the conversion back to the exact click and campaign.
Conversion timestamp Exact time the lifecycle event occurred. Must be within 90 days of the original click or Google will reject the import.
Payment status Whether the account has made a first payment. Lets Google's Smart Bidding distinguish a paying conversion from a free signup.
Activation status Whether the account has completed activation (onboarded, set up, first meaningful action). A leading signal before payment that helps Google optimise earlier in the funnel.
Enriched account info Account name and firmographic data — company size, industry, geography. Enables Google to build lookalike audiences from the profile of accounts that actually converted, not just from email hashes.

Google matches the GCLID to the original ad click and credits the campaign. With enriched firmographic signals, Smart Bidding can optimise for the type of account that converts — not just the volume of conversions.

How does ThriveStack correlate a Google Ads click to a paying customer?

The GCLID is the thread that ties every step together. ThriveStack keeps it attached to the visitor record from first click all the way through to payment — then hands it back to Google so the campaign gets credited.

1
Sarah clicks a Google Ad

Google's auto-tagging appends a unique GCLID to the destination URL:

fivex.com/?gclid=ABC999
2
ThriveStack captures the GCLID on landing

The tracking script reads gclid=ABC999 from the URL on the very first page load and stores it against an anonymous visitor ID:

visitor_id: V123
gclid: ABC999
3
Sarah signs up — GCLID transfers to her identity

When Sarah creates an account, ThriveStack merges the anonymous session into the identified user record. The GCLID travels with it:

user_id: U456 ← linked to V123
email: sarah@company.com
gclid: ABC999 ← still attached
4
Sarah pays — ThriveStack fires a conversion event

A first_payment event is recorded. ThriveStack looks up U456, finds the attached GCLID, and sends the conversion to Google's Offline Conversions API:

gclid: ABC999
conversion: first_payment
timestamp: 2026-04-28T14:32:00Z
5
Google closes the loop

Google matches ABC999 back to the original Spring Campaign click. The campaign is credited with the conversion. Smart Bidding now knows this campaign drives paying customers and adjusts bids accordingly.

The GCLID is the only shared key between Google Ads and ThriveStack. Without it — for example if auto-tagging is off, or the GCLID was not captured before a redirect — the conversion cannot be attributed back to the campaign.

How long before Google starts optimizing bids based on ThriveStack conversion data?

Google's Smart Bidding algorithms need at least 30–50 conversions per month per campaign to exit "learning mode" and start making meaningful bid adjustments.

In practice, expect two phases after go-live:

  • Weeks 1–4: Google collects conversion data but makes few bid changes. You'll see conversions appearing in Google Ads reports — verify the counts match ThriveStack.
  • Weeks 4–8: Once enough conversions accumulate, Smart Bidding begins shifting budget toward higher-converting segments. Campaigns with lower volume take longer.

For B2B SaaS with longer sales cycles, prioritize sending demo_booked as a leading indicator — it occurs more frequently than first_payment and gives Google faster signal to learn from.

What happens if a user clicks an ad on mobile but converts on desktop?

This is a known limitation of all offline conversion systems — not specific to ThriveStack. Here's how ThriveStack handles it:

  • Same email on both devices (identity merge): When the user signs up on desktop with the same email they used on mobile, ThriveStack merges the two sessions. The GCLID from the mobile session is carried over to the identified user record — the conversion is sent to Google correctly.
  • Devices never linked: If the user never logs in on the mobile device, the two sessions cannot be merged. The conversion is recorded in ThriveStack but sent to Google without a GCLID — it won't appear in Google Ads attribution.

In ThriveStack's reports, you can see total conversions vs. attributed conversions to understand the cross-device gap for your audience.

Will Customer Match lists work if we have fewer than 1,000 customers?

Google requires a Customer Match list to have at least 1,000 matched users before the audience becomes usable in campaigns. If your list is below this threshold:

  • Google will accept the upload without error
  • The audience will show as "Too small to use" or display <1000 in Google Ads Audience Manager
  • Raynor cannot target or exclude this audience in campaigns until the threshold is met

Recommended approach for early-stage accounts:

  • Start with the free trial segment — it's typically larger and more likely to clear 1,000 users first
  • Add the paying customers exclusion list once you have enough volume
  • ThriveStack logs a warning when uploading lists under 500 users so you're never surprised
Do we need to change our existing UTM setup?

You don't need to remove existing UTMs, but two changes will improve data quality:

1. Switch campaign IDs to campaign names

If you're currently passing numeric IDs in utm_campaign (e.g. 18492837), switch to readable names:

utm_campaign=q2_linkedin_awareness_april

ThriveStack displays this string as-is in all reports — numeric IDs make attribution reports unreadable.

2. Keep auto-tagging ON for Google Ads

GCLIDs (added automatically by Google auto-tagging) are required for offline conversion import. UTMs on Google Ads links are optional — useful as a readable label in ThriveStack reports but not a substitute for GCLID. For all non-Google channels (LinkedIn, email, newsletters), UTMs are your only attribution mechanism and must be set.

What happens to conversion data if there's a long gap between an ad click and the actual conversion?

Google enforces a hard 90-day window: conversion events must be sent within 90 days of the original ad click. After that, Google will reject the import for that GCLID.

How ThriveStack handles this:

  • Every queued conversion event is stamped with the original GCLID capture timestamp
  • Events where now − gclid_captured_at > 88 days are discarded rather than sent (to avoid confusing Google API errors)
  • An alert fires when events are dropped this way — so your team knows attribution was lost
  • The conversion still appears in ThriveStack's own reports regardless of GCLID expiry

For enterprise B2B deals with sales cycles longer than 3 months, consider using demo_booked as your primary Google Ads conversion — it occurs well within the 90-day window while first_payment may not.
