Session 2
Instrumenting Feature Usage ~1 hr

Instrument feature_used events for every meaningful action in your product. ThriveStack classifies features into three types — core, sticky, and support — and uses that classification to power feature adoption and retention analytics.

1

List & Classify Your Features

~15 min

Before writing any code, list every meaningful user action in your product and agree on a type with your product team. Getting this right upfront ensures your analytics reflect how users actually experience the product.

Type Definition Example
core The primary reason a user signs up and pays Creating a report, running an analysis
sticky Drives repeated engagement and habit formation Viewing a dashboard, checking notifications
support Utility and configuration actions Updating settings, connecting an integration

Every feature maps to exactly one type. If you're unsure, the ThriveStack team can help you classify during a guided session.

2

Instrument the Code

~30 min–1 hr

Give your AI coding assistant the feature list you classified in step 1. The AI locates each feature's interaction handler in the codebase and adds the correct feature_used call with the right type and name.

AI locates each feature's handler No manual file hunting — the prompt instructs the AI to search for the interaction point for each feature you listed.
Fires feature_used with correct type and name feature_name in [noun]_[verb] format, feature_type set to your classification, user_id and context.group_id attached.
Self-verifies coverage The AI produces a summary table confirming every feature from your list has been instrumented.

Session 3
Setting Up Activation Journeys ~1 hr

Map your onboarding flow, instrument the right events, then configure the activation journey inside ThriveStack. This session unlocks activation rate tracking, step-by-step funnel analysis, and automatic alerting for accounts stuck before reaching value.

1

Understand Your Product Onboarding Journey

~15 min

Before writing any code, map the sequence of steps a new user must complete to reach their first moment of value. This becomes the source of truth for what ThriveStack tracks and reports on.

What is the first action a new user takes after signing up?
What are the discrete steps between signup and value?
What is the aha moment — the point where a user first experiences the product's core value?
Do users go through onboarding individually or as a team?

Write these steps down in order — you'll use this list in steps 2 and 3. The ThriveStack team can help you identify and sequence your aha moment during a guided session.

2

Instrument the Onboarding Events

~30 min–1 hr

Provide the AI with your ordered list of onboarding steps. It locates the completion handler for each step and adds the correct event call, including invite_sent wherever team invitations are triggered.

onboarding_step_completed per step Fired after each discrete onboarding action succeeds, with step_name matching the names from your journey map.
invite_sent at every invitation point Fired whenever a user sends a team invitation — a key virality and activation signal.
AI self-verifies step coverage The AI produces a summary confirming every step from your list is instrumented before handing back control.

Guided Onboarding

1 session  ·  ~30 min with ThriveStack team

Book session
Setup with AI — full documentation

For each step in your journey map, locate its completion handler and fire the event after the action succeeds. Use consistent step_name values — these are what ThriveStack uses to build your funnel.

Fire onboarding_step_completed per step Include step_name, user_id, and context.group_id. Use the same step names you'll enter in the ThriveStack dashboard.
Fire invite_sent at every invitation entry point Covers in-app invite forms, bulk imports, and any SSO or admin-triggered additions.
Never fire before the action succeeds Place event calls after the server confirms success or the UI transitions — not on button click.

Session 4
Validating the Instrumentation End to End ~30 min

Deploy to production and confirm every event is arriving correctly. Use ThriveStack's built-in tooling to validate reports, review your account list, and set yourself up to debug quickly in the future.

1

Validate the Reports

~15 min

With your instrumentation live, open the core Product Intelligence reports and confirm they reflect real data.

  • Activation Report — verify user activation rates and funnel drop-off points are being tracked.
  • Feature Adoption Report — confirm core, sticky, and support features are registering usage.
  • Retention / Cohort Report — check that account-level cohorts are forming correctly.
  • Setup Checklist — open the ThriveStack setup checklist and confirm all lifecycle and feature events are green.
2

Validate the Account List

~10 min

Review your account and user list to ensure identity resolution is working as expected.

  • Open the Accounts view — confirm accounts created during signup are appearing.
  • Verify users are correctly linked to their accounts (user-to-account association).
  • Check that account traits (plan, company name, etc.) are populated if you're sending them.
  • Confirm no duplicate or anonymous accounts are appearing unexpectedly.
3

Debugging in the Future

Reference

When something looks off, use these built-in ThriveStack tools to diagnose and fix issues fast.

Diagnostics Report A health-check report that flags missing events, broken identity calls, or instrumentation gaps. Start here when something isn't showing up.
Audit Logs A full record of every API call ThriveStack has received — useful for verifying payloads, timestamps, and event names.
Live Events Stream A real-time feed of incoming events. Trigger an action in your app and watch it appear here instantly — ideal for testing during development.

Open Diagnostics Report

Your first stop when events are missing or reports look wrong.
